COPPA (CHILDREN ONLINE PRIVACY PROTECTION ACT)


When it comes to the collection of personal information from children under 13, the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) puts parents in control. The Federal Trade Commission, the nation's consumer protection agency, enforces the COPPA Rule, which spells out what operators of websites and online services must do to protect children's privacy and safety online.


We do not specifically market to children under 13, but PII for children under 13 may be collected and stored within OpenLearn. OpenLearn complies with COPPA as outlined here as well as in Sections “Operators Collecting Information” and “Information That We Collect”. As independent organizations, non-Credera Operators will determine their specific methods for COPPA compliance, following the guidelines below.


Because collected information will be used exclusively for internal purposes of Operator organizational management and will not be disclosed or otherwise transferred to outside parties, Operators may collect PII prior to obtaining verifiable parental consent in order to send direct notice to parents on the privacy policy and the child’s participation in the Operator organization. The direct notice to parents will:



  • State that the operator has collected the parent’s online contact information from the child in order to provide notice to, and subsequently update the parent about, a child’s participation in a website or online service that does not otherwise collect, use, or disclose children’s personal information;

  • State that the parent’s online contact information will not be used or disclosed for any other purpose;

  • State that the parent may refuse to permit the child’s participation in the website or online service and may require the deletion of the parent’s online contact information, and how the parent can do so; and

  • Provide a hyperlink to the operator’s online notice of its information practices


After sending parents a direct notice, Operators will obtain verifiable parental consent using any of the methods as outlined in COPPA below:


  • Providing a consent form to be signed by the parent and returned via U.S. mail, fax, or electronic scan (the “print-and-send” method)

  • Requiring the parent, in connection with a monetary transaction, to use a credit card, debit card, or other online payment system that provides notification of each discrete transaction to the primary account holder;

  • Having the parent call a toll-free telephone number staffed by trained personnel, or have the parent connect to trained personnel via video-conference; or

  • Verifying a parent’s identity by checking a form of government-issued identification against databases of such information, provided that you promptly delete the parent’s identification after completing the verification. 

  • Requesting that the parent indicate consent in the return message to the Operator’s direct notice containing this Privacy Policy; the Operator will take an additional confirming step after receiving the parent’s message that may be:

    • Requesting in Operator’s initial message to the parent that the parent include a phone or fax number or mailing address in the reply message, so that the Operator can follow up with a confirming phone call, fax or letter to the parent

    • After a reasonable time delay, sending another message via the parent’s online contact information to confirm consent.  In this confirmatory message, the Operator should include all the original information contained in the direct notice, inform the parent that he or she can revoke the consent, and inform the parent how to do so.
